Text Annotation

Text annotation is the process of adding information or metadata to textual data to enhance its understanding, relevance, or usability. This additional information can include labels, tags, or other types of annotations that provide context, meaning, or structure to the text. Text annotation is widely used in natural language processing (NLP), machine learning, and data analysis applications to train models, improve search results, and make sense of unstructured text data.

Text Summarization

Text summarization is the process of creating a concise and condensed version of a given text while retaining its key information and meaning. The goal of text summarization is to generate a shorter representation of a document, article, or piece of text, making it more accessible and easier to comprehend without losing the essential content.

WordCloud

A word cloud is a visual representation of text data, where words from a given body of text are displayed in a graphical manner, with the size of each word indicating its frequency or importance. In a word cloud, more frequently occurring words are usually displayed in larger and bolder fonts, making them stand out. Word clouds are often used to quickly and visually summarize the most prominent terms within a text, helping to identify key themes, keywords, or trends. They are commonly employed in various fields, including data analysis, text mining, and information visualization.

Text to Speech

Text-to-Speech (TTS) is a technology that converts written text into spoken language. It's a form of speech synthesis where a computer or a device processes the text input and produces corresponding audio output. The goal of TTS is to enable machines to "read" text content aloud, making it accessible to users who may prefer listening or have visual impairments.

Speech to Text

Speech to Text (STT), also known as Automatic Speech Recognition (ASR), is a technology that converts spoken language into written text. This process involves transcribing spoken words into a written format, making it possible to convert audio data, such as speech from a conversation, lecture, or recorded audio file, into a text representation.
